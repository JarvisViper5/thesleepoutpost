The Ancient Library of Alexandria a Journey through Time

A sleep story for adults · Designed to help you fall asleep · ~45 minutes

Read the Opening

Tonight, we are going to travel somewhere extraordinary. Somewhere ancient and beautiful and utterly peaceful. We are going to visit the most magnificent library the world has ever known, the Great Library of Alexandria, jewel of the ancient Mediterranean world, home of all human wisdom, where scholars from every corner of the earth once walked in reverent silence among scrolls so numerous they could not be counted.

But we will not be rushing. We will not be hurrying toward any destination. There is no lesson to learn tonight, no knowledge to acquire. We will simply arrive, slowly and gently, and wander through those legendary halls with all the time in the world. We will breathe the air that those ancient scholars breathed. We will touch, with our minds, the things they touched with their hands. We will let the deep, settled peace of that place move through us like warm water.

There is nothing to do tonight. Nothing to understand. Nothing to remember. Only to drift, gently and freely, through one of the most beautiful places that ever existed in human memory.,

Not a grand ship, a modest trading vessel of ancient cedar wood, its single sail of cream colored linen full and easy in the afternoon breeze. The ship moves slowly across the water, its movement barely perceptible, a gentle, continuous rocking, as steady and reliable as breathing. The sea around you is a deep, luminous blue, the kind of blue that seems to hold light inside it, like a sapphire held up to the afternoon sun, glowing from within.

The day is warm and unhurried. A gentle breeze comes off the water, carrying with it the faint salt smell of the sea and something else, something green and ancient, like the smell of lands where civilization has lived for thousands of years. The oarsmen move in a slow, rhythmic stroke. Pull through the water... and rest. Pull... and rest. The sound of their oars dipping and rising is steady and measured and endlessly calming, like a heartbeat, like the ticking of a very old and reliable clock.

You are sitting at the bow of the ship, facing forward, wrapped in a soft cloth of undyed wool. The afternoon sun is warm on your face and arms. The deck beneath you is smooth and warm too, worn soft by years of sea and weather, and you are perfectly comfortable...
