A Sleeper Train Drifting through the Rings of Saturn Carryin

A sleep story for adults · Designed to help you fall asleep · ~45 minutes

Read the Opening

There is a place beyond the edge of everything you know, where the stars gather like droplets of silver on velvet and the darkness hums with ancient songs. There, suspended in the infinite quiet of the outer solar system, a platform waits. It has no name that anyone remembers, though once, perhaps, someone etched letters into its weathered surface. Those letters have long since faded into the soft amber glow that permeates this place, a gentle luminescence that seems to emanate from the very fabric of space itself. You find yourself standing on this platform, though you do not remember how you arrived. Your feet rest upon planks of wood that feel impossibly warm beneath you, as if the sun itself once touched them, long ago, and they have never forgotten that warmth.

Above you, Saturn rises in endless grandeur, a pale gold sphere veiled in mist and mystery. Its rings stretch outward like a divine harmony made visible, bands of ice and dust and light that ripple and shimmer with colors you cannot name. The rings do not spin hurriedly; they orbit with the patience of eternity.

A sound reaches you, faint at first, then growing slowly like dawn itself. It is the sound of wheels turning, of metal singing against metal. The train emerges from the haze between the stars, crafted from dark timber polished to a satin sheen, with windows that glow with warm amber light.

Take a breath, slow and deep. Let it out even slower. You are safe here. You are welcome here.

Inside, you find deep burgundy velvet seats and reading lamps that cast pools of golden light. You choose a window seat, feeling your body loosen, each knot releasing its hold like ice melting in spring sunshine. The conductor offers you a small cup of something warm and fragrant. The liquid within is the color of amber, of honey held to light. You do not know what it is, yet you know it is safe. You know it is good.

The motion is so gentle you might not notice it if you were not paying attention. Through your window, you watch the platform recede until it is nothing at all. The space around you opens infinitely. Saturn hangs above, beside, around you, a presence rather than a planet, a consciousness of golden light and ancient gravity.
