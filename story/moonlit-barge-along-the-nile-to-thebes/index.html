Moonlit Barge along the Nile to Thebes

A sleep story for adults · Designed to help you fall asleep · ~45 minutes

Read the Opening

The night deepens, but the story only becomes softer. You pass beneath archways where carvings of stars and leaves have been smoothed by countless hands over countless years. You sit on a smooth stone bench while bronze bowls catching amber firelight settles into bronze bowls catching amber firelight, and the path ahead remains easy and clear. In the distance, you hear a gate chain settle, then silence again, thick and comforting. This hour belongs to slowness, to release, to letting the body understand that it can finally unclench. If thoughts appear, let them pass like lanterns drifting down a slow river at night.

Ahead of you, a long corridor of columns gives way to an open court where night settles like a silk veil. You move through the avenue at an easy pace, listening to soft river mist moving between columns while the scent of olive leaves shifting in a light breeze drifts through the air. Somewhere nearby, someone closes a wooden gate with care, and the sound fades almost as soon as it arrives. Nothing urgent follows it. The calm returns immediately, deeper than before. The stones beneath your steps still hold a little warmth from the day, and that warmth seems to rise into your feet and legs, easing every small point of tension. If thoughts appear, let them pass like lanterns drifting down a slow river at night.

You enter a quieter stretch of the journey where shadows and firelight move together in slow patterns. Here, details become soothing: the grain of cedar beams above you, the curve of clay vessels along a wall, the way fabric stirs when a gentle wind crosses the courtyard. You follow a shaded colonnade while lantern reflections gliding across still water blends with soft river mist moving between columns, and your breathing finds the same steady cadence as the scene around you. You do not need to remember anything for later. You can simply be here now, in this calm unfolding hour. If thoughts appear, let them pass like lanterns drifting down a slow river at night.

Now the journey opens onto a terrace where the sky is wide and dark blue, scattered with stars that appear close enough to touch. Below, rooftops and courtyards rest in layers of amber light. You trace old inscriptions with your fingertips and let your gaze move without effort from one soft point of light...
