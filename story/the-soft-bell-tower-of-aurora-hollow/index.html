The Soft Bell Tower of Aurora Hollow

A sleep story for adults · Designed to help you fall asleep · ~45 minutes

Read the Opening

Ahead of you, the path widens and the light softens. You let your breathing settle, listening to the smallest sounds while moonlight on glass bridges blends into moonlight on glass bridges. Your steps become quieter, your jaw unclenches, and your shoulders drop as the story keeps an easy rhythm. Let your shoulders soften and let your breath come and go without effort.

In this part of the night, you walk slowly through the soft bell tower of aurora hollow, moving at a pace so gentle that even restless thoughts begin to slow. The air carries soft chimes in the sleeping trees, and every turn opens into moonlight on glass bridges, as if the whole world is breathing with you. Nothing urgent follows you here. Nothing asks anything from you except presence. With every exhale, feel your body grow a little heavier, a little quieter, a little more at rest.

The night deepens, but only into calm. You notice how gently everything moves, how each breath takes a little less effort than the one before. You are held by the steadiness of the scene and by the softness of this hour. You are safe here, held by the steady pace of the story and the gentle rhythm of the night.
