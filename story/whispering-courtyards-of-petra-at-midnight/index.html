Whispering Courtyards of Petra at Midnight

A sleep story for adults · Designed to help you fall asleep · ~45 minutes

Read the Opening

The night deepens, but the story only becomes softer. You pass beneath archways where carvings of stars and leaves have been smoothed by countless hands over countless years. You sit on a smooth stone bench while cedar smoke and distant incense settles into low bells traveling from a far temple, and the path ahead remains easy and clear. In the distance, you hear a gate chain settle, then silence again, thick and comforting. This hour belongs to slowness, to release, to letting the body understand that it can finally unclench. Let your shoulders soften and let your breath come and go without effort.

You enter a quieter stretch of the journey where shadows and firelight move together in slow patterns. Here, details become soothing: the grain of cedar beams above you, the curve of clay vessels along a wall, the way fabric stirs when a gentle wind crosses the courtyard. You trace old inscriptions with your fingertips while cool marble under a steady night sky blends with bronze bowls catching amber firelight, and your breathing finds the same steady cadence as the scene around you. You do not need to remember anything for later. You can simply be here now, in this calm unfolding hour. Let your shoulders soften and let your breath come and go without effort.

Now the journey opens onto a terrace where the sky is wide and dark blue, scattered with stars that appear close enough to touch. Below, rooftops and courtyards rest in layers of amber light. You walk slowly and let your gaze move without effort from one soft point of light to another. A cool breeze brushes your face and then passes on. The city below continues its quiet breathing, and you continue yours, matched to it, steady and light. Let your shoulders soften and let your breath come and go without effort.

Now the journey opens onto a terrace where the sky is wide and dark blue, scattered with stars that appear close enough to touch. Below, rooftops and courtyards rest in layers of amber light. You rest beside a fountain and let your gaze move without effort from one soft point of light to another. A cool breeze brushes your face and then passes on.
